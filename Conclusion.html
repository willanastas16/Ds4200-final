Conclusion

The Polaris Data Insights Report and Global Estimates of Modern Slavery provide key contextual evidence and data to support our analysis of the CTDC dataset. Below are the insights, challenges, and actionable recommendations derived from this project:

  • Data Insights: The static and interactive visualizations reveal clear global, gender, and temporal disparities in trafficking data.
  • Challenges: Significant data gaps and underreporting, particularly for minority genders and less visible exploitation forms.
  • Actionable Insights: Focus efforts on regions and demographics with higher trafficking risks, leveraging data to guide interventions.
  • Call to Action: NGOs and governments must prioritize comprehensive data collection and targeted victim support systems.
  • Expand Data Scope: Enhance data collection processes to include marginalized groups and regions.
  • Policy Implementation: Research gender-specific and region-specific anti-trafficking policies.
  • Interactive Tools: Build on interactive visualizations to create dashboards for specific high-risk countries.
